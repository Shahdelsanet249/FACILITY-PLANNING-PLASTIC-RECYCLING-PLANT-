# Plastic Recycling Plant: Industrial Engineering & Facilities Design

## 📌 Overview
A comprehensive presentation on the design and implementation of a plastic recycling facility from an industrial engineering and facilities design perspective. This project presents a systems-level approach to optimizing throughput, machine flow, and spatial organization within a modern recycling plant.

## 🎯 Project Objectives
- *Transform end-of-life plastics* into high-grade, reusable manufacturing materials
- *Achieve environmental sustainability* while delivering economic value
- *Apply best engineering practices* in production optimization and worker safety
- *Establish a circular economy* model for plastic waste management

## 🏭 Five Core Operational Pillars

The facility design is built on these five interdependent pillars:

### 1. *Continuous Production Flow*
- Minimizing idle time and work-in-progress (WIP) bottlenecks
- Optimized scheduling and material sequencing
- Just-in-time (JIT) processing principles

### 2. *Automated Material Handling*
- Reducing manual touchpoints to increase safety
- Conveyor systems and robotic material transfer
- Scalable throughput with minimal labor variability

### 3. *Energy-Efficient Operations*
- Optimizing thermal and mechanical power draw
- Variable frequency drives (VFDs) for equipment
- Heat recovery and waste minimization systems

### 4. *Quality Control Systems*
- Real-time monitoring and defect detection
- Strict adherence to manufacturer standards
- Automated sorting and material purity verification

### 5. *Safe Working Environment*
- Ergonomic layout separating operators from heavy machinery
- Hazard zoning and personal protective equipment (PPE) protocols
- Compliance with OSHA and ISO safety standards
